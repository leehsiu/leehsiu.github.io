My research interests lie broadly in computer vision and machine learning. I'm now focusing on 3D vision including neural rendering, and neural avatar reconstruction. I also spare some time on low-level vision including deblur and super-resolution. The goal of my research is to bridge our real-world perception and the digital world by faithfully capturing, manipulating and presenting our real world.

Short Bio (cv)

    Xiu Li is a Senior Ph.D. student (from Aug. 2015) in the Department of Automation at Tsinghua University, advised by Prof. Qionghai Dai and a Research Intern at MSRA (from Mar.2021). He was a visiting scholar in School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University from Sep. 2017 to Aug, 2019, working with Prof.Yaser Sheikh and Prof. Hongdong Li. Before that, Xiu received B.E. in Department of Automation,Tsinghua University. His research spans from 3D Vision to computational photography and machine learning. He was with the champion team of 2013 International Aerial Robotics Competition.
